Salting the password

Salting a passwords makes it a little harder to decrypt the hashed version of a password, the way it works is your pretty much adding more data to a users password making it less likely to appear in a rainbow table. For example, the password "123456" is fairly likely to be in a rainbow table, but the salted version "123456webfjdirifnskrodmwjrmfkdiejtmfndkiojreoijviojwfeoijregiojvoidjg" is less likely to be in a rainbow table.

Multiple hash passes

The more passes you make, the longer it takes someone to unhash your passwords. It's recommended you make as many passes as possible without hurting your performance too much. I made the code I wrote year based, so if the user last updated their password in 2012 they will have more passes than a user who last updated in 2005.

Ditching MD5

I first started using MD5 back in 2005, so it's no surprise it's not considered a top notch hashing method. Your better using the one I used. Try updating your hashing method based on the year, that way your for the most part very up to date.
