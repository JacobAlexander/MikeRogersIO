A few weeks ago I was pointed towards a post by Steve Corona where he discussed living without time. I found the idea pretty interesting, so decided to give it a try while working on a few project sprints.

Removing time

Avoiding time was damn hard.

I normally work on a Mac, so I found a neat little app called Bartender which hides items from your menu bar.  It's really good, I removed a bunch of items from my bar which I either didn't care for or could tell me the time. Here is my current menu bar:

My phone is my primary timepiece (I don't own a watch), so it seemed like it would make sense to remove the clock from the lock screen. Unfortunately I couldn't find any decent apps, instead I just switched my phone to no-vibrate mode & left it facing down for my work session.

The last thing I changed was my music. I was pretty sure I would notice the gaps between songs & use them as a point of reference. To avoid this I increase the crossfade on my music to 5 seconds & put on a trance playlist.

First sprint

One of the first things I noticed when I removed clocks from a coding session, was how much easier it was to focus on a task in hand. I'm not sure why, but the amount of time I procrastinated on reddit/Hacker news decreased a little.

Losing my time reference is fun.
